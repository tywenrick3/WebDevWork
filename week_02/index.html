Bush pushes the idea that using technology to expand our minds is the key to thriving. I found the example with the store to be extremely convincing. In Long Live The Web by Tim Burners Lee, I found the ending to be extraordinarily interesting. Lee talks about the future of the web and what he is excited for. He explains how as of right now, the web is a place where data is being uploaded at an extraordinarily rate. Documents are constantly being added, with the ability to be seen by anybody. But as of right now the machine is not truly able to interact with the data. And so much is affected by this. As computers begin to be able to interact with the data and documents that populate the web, so many new possibilities for advancement arise. I began thinking about all of the underground and unpopular discoveries that may not be so lucky to make it to mainstream media. Or fake news that surfaces with nothing backing its claims. As we are able to fix this problem and create more ways for computer interaction to be possible. So many new areas for improvement and progress will begin to emerge.

Tim Burners Lee
